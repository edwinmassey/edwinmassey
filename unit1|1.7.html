Unit 1 Outcome 1.7

Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

Introduction

GDPR stands for general data protection regulations. This regulation was implemented on 25th May 2018 and this is all about protecting personal data online. Personal data includes information such as your name, email, banking information and IP address.

Main features of GDPR

These are the main features of this regulation, users will have the right to:
Examine and check - users are allowed to see how their personal data is being used within the business and have the right to request to see their data.
Delete - if a person doesn’t want to have their data stored within the business no more they have the right to delete that data.
Transfer - users are allowed to transfer their data from one business to another.
Notified - individuals have to be informed when their personal data is being gathered by a business. Users must opt in to allow their data to be stored. Also if their data has been breached within the business they must be notified within 72 hours.
Update - Users have the right to update any data that is no longer valid or out of date which they want to update.
Not be used - A user can requests for their data not to be used for processing then they have the right to do so. They can allow for their data to be stored but not used.

The impact it will have

This regulation will impact digital projects as for example if you are creating a website that will store personal data then you need to comply with the regulation otherwise you will face a serious fine and have your business reputation impacted. This could result in users not trusting you leading to them not using your website.

How to keep within guidelines

In order to keep within the guidelines before users input personal data let them read the terms and conditions of what data you are going to store. Provide them information of what data you are going to store, how you are going to use and process their data and the security measures put in place to keep their data safe. Allow users to agree to opt in letting them know they agree to have their data being used by the business. To reduce the risks of not complying with the guidelines you can minimise how much data you are storing by only having users input necessary data.
