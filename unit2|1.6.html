Unit 2 Outcome 1.6

1.6 Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can overcome them.

Introduction

There are many users online using websites that may have a disability such as visual impairments, learning difficulties or hearing loss. When creating a website it is important to make our website as accessible as possible for users.

Alt tags

For users with visual impairments seeing images on our website may be difficult for them. They may struggle knowing what the image is about, this is where alt tags is very helpful as for any image we put on our website adding alt tags that describe what the image is will allow users to read the alt tags to explain the image. Users may be using a screen reader that reads text out loud on their computer so with alt tag they can hover over the image and have the alt tag read out loud to them.

Keeping simple

We may have users using our website who have learning disabilities where reading can be difficult for them. Keeping our text into paragraphs with subheadings will allow readers to quickly find the information they are looking for without needing to read a large amount of text. Using straight forward language will also help be more accessible for a larger audience.

Colour and layout

Some users on our website may be colour blind so it is important to use a colour style that is easy to read. Using a red background with pink text will be very difficult to see but using a white background with black text is easy to read. Deciding the right colour choice is important to be accessible as possible.
